Types of Smoke

Once our Technicians arrive to the sight of the damage we will test the soot to determine which type of smoke damage occurred. Cleaning procedures will be based on the information identified during fire damage assessment. We have the capability to respond to any property damage situation on a 24-hour basis and respond with emergency board-ups, water extractions, temporary roof repairs, odor control, and air filtration using specialized industry equipment. If you experience a fire in your home, give us a call as soon as you can.

Types of soot include:

  • Wet Smoke Residues - Result from smoldering fires with low heat. Residues are sticky, smeary and with pungent odors. Smoke webs can be difficult to clean.
  • Dry Smoke Residues - Result from fast burning fires at high temperatures. Residues are often dry, powdery, small, non-smeary smoke particulates.
  • Protein Residues - Virtually invisible residues that discolor paints and varnishes. Extreme pungent odor.
  • Other Types of Residues - Tear gas, fingerprint powder, and fire extinguisher residues also need cleanup.

Home Owner Tips

  • Do not disturb the area around the damaged source or move items.
  • Do not turn on your homes electricity.
  • Do not attempt to clean or wipe and wash fire residue from walls, ceilings or other absorbent surfaces as this can further damage these areas.
  • Call your insurance company immediately.
  • Take immediate action by evacuating all personnel from the premises.
