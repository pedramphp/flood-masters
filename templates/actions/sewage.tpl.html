Home Owner Tips

  • Sewage overflow from common water reservoirs are not clean and contain millions of microorganisms and other contaminants.
  • Certain materials like carpets that were in direct contact with sewage may not be saved.
  • Chlorine or bleach is not enough to treat sewage or kills bacteria in sewage.
  • Keep children and pets away from sewage-contaminated areas as they may be vulnerable to health risks.
  • Avoid direct contact with sewage by wearing the proper clothing, which includes masks, respirators, rubber gloves, goggles, rubber boots, and safety helmets.
  • Always handle with care all products that encountered direct contract with sewage.
  • Surfaces affected by sewage should be treated as contaminated areas and should be avoided until proper sewage remediation is completed and the area disinfected.
  • Partially flooded homes and other establishments are not safe from the dangers of sewage.
