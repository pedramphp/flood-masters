Carpet Cleaning

Hot Water Extraction/Steam Cleaning: The most common type of residential and commercial carpet cleaning method. It is recommended by most carpet and fiber manufacturers such as; Shaw Industries, Mohawk, Masland, Fabrica, Monsanto and Dupont. This method can be done by portable or truck-mounted equipment by spraying heated cleaning solution into the carpet pile while simultaneously extracting the dirt and solution with a powerful vacuum - never allowing the carpet to get soaked.

The Hot Water Extraction method can be very effective provided the following rules are followed:

  • A non-soap cleaner is used so that no soapy residue is left behind to attract dirt to the carpet.
  • Solution temperature is maintained for optimal cleaning. Typically 150 degrees or higher is optimal.
  • The pressure of the solution being sprayed onto the carpet high enough to penetrate the carpet fibers deep down.
  • The Vacuum power of the machine is sufficient to remove the majority of the cleaning solution and soil particles. Rental units are NOT recommended since most are considered inferior to what a professional uses.

METHODS OF AREA RUG CLEANING

Typical Hand-Washed Rug Cleaning Procedure:
  • Inspect areas prior to cleaning.
  • Dust rugs to remove dry soils.
  • Pre-spot for any oil or grease stains.
  • Immerse rugs in cold water to loosen and suspend soils deep within the rugs.
  • Hand shampoo with approved cleaning detergents by the Wools of New Zealand.
  • Hand scrub soiled fringes.
  • Thoroughly rinse all soils and detergents of rugs with cold water.
  • Wring out rugs and remove excess moisture with high volume water extractor.
  • Groom nap for uniformity and dry in climate controlled drying room.
  • Brush, vacuum and post-spot any stubborn spots for maximum results.

Sewage Clean Up

When a sewage disaster occurs in a home, it is critical that the affected area is cleaned and disinfected to minimize the potential for disease and other airborne pollutants. It is important to know that many items that have sewage damage are most likely going to become a total lost. Depending on the type of porous material and level of contamination, many items should be discarded (mattresses, pillows, baby toys, paper products) are most likely going to become a total loss unless the item can be cleaned through a special process involving hot water and cleaning chemicals.

In most cases cleaning up sewage disasters involves removing the carpeting and drywall in the affected areas. Once the sewage has been cleaned and the affected materials removed, the disinfection process begins. Using specialized cleaning solutions help to kill bacteria and disinfect the affected areas from sewage damages.

Our water damage restoration team has extensive experience in cleaning properties from water damage disasters. If your property has suffered from a sewage damage disaster, we will attempt to minimize damages and contain impacted areas when possible. You can feel confident our goal is to return your property back to normal as quickly and safely as possible.
